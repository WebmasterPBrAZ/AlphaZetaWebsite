- What We Do

"Phi Boota roota is a professional fraternity that attempts to instill a feelings of brotherhood amongst percussionists across the nation."

We, as brothers assist in bringing the percussionists at Troy University closer together, through many means. We help motivate each other to practice, and to push each other to become better players. We help build, purchase, and support anything the percussion studio needs. When the school of music holds functions such as SEUS, concert performances for concert bands, and percussion ensemble performances, PBr helps move whatever percussion equipment is necessary. Below is a list of services PBr normally commits to, as well as recently completed, and upcoming services, for the school of music, and the community.

- Services

Football Games- Our chapter assists in making sure the drumline has their appropriate stands and stand risers at home games for in the stands playing.

Marching SEUS- We will raffle off a drumhead signed by the 'SOTS' drumline to help pay for much needed equipment in the percussion studio here at Troy, as well as assist in moving pit equippment for the competing high school bands.

MS and HS SEUS- Alpha Zeta brothers will move equipment for the three respective bands, and move equipment as needed, as well as make sure the instruments needed are available for the performance.

University Performances- Whenever there is a studio, recital, or concert performance with individuals or groups in the percussion studio, brothers of the AZ chapter will move equipment and make sure they are in the appropriate location for the intended performance.

Studio Maintnence - When needed, AZ brothers will help make sure the percussion studio is in tip top shape and is cleaned up after long semesters of use, as well as help purchase, build, and repair instruments, as well as building, and maintaining cabinets used to house instruments both in the studio and for performance tours.

Set up and put away- During the spring semester, brothers will help in setting up equipment needed for the concert bands to rehearse, as well as help put instruments away after all the rehearsals have ended. This is invaluable to keeping our equipment in proper working conditions and to prevent people not of the studio or who are not brothers from accidenally damaging any equipment.
